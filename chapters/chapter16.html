16. Biodiversity & Conservation

Preserving the library of life.

"Extinction is forever."

Key Takeaways

  • Biodiversity = variety of life at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels
  • Three types: Genetic diversity, Species diversity, Ecosystem diversity
  • Threats: Habitat loss, overexploitation, invasive species, climate change
  • Conservation strategies: In-situ (National Parks) and Ex-situ (Zoos, Gene Banks)

1. Levels of Biodiversity

1. Genetic Diversity

Variation within a species (genes).
Ex: 50,000 varieties of rice in India. Disease resistance depends on this.

2. Species Diversity

Variety of species in a region (Richness).
Ex: Western Ghats has more amphibians than Eastern Ghats.

3. Ecosystem Diversity

Variety of habitats (deserts, rainforests, mangroves, coral reefs) in an area.
Ex: India has great ecosystem diversity compared to Norway.

2. The Data Vault: Conservation Status

IUCN Red List Categories.

Category Criteria Example
Extinct (EX) No individuals left worldwide. Dodo, Passenger Pigeon, Asiatic Cheetah (in India).
Critically Endangered (CR) Extremely high risk in wild (90% decline). Immediate threat. Great Indian Bustard, Javan Rhino, Gharial.
Endangered (EN) Very high risk (70% decline). Bengal Tiger, Blue Whale, Red Panda.
Vulnerable (VU) High risk. Snow Leopard, African Elephant, Dugong.
The 'Evil Quartet' (HIPPO) - Drivers of Loss
  • H: Habitat Loss (Deforestation). (Biggest Threat: 90% impact).
  • I: Invasive Species (Water Hyacinth, Lantana, Eucalyptus). Kill natives.
  • P: Pollution (Pesticides, Plastics).
  • P: Population Growth (Human resource demand).
  • O: Over-exploitation (Hunting, Poaching, Overfishing).

Biodiversity Hotspots

Regions with High Endemism (unique species) + High Threat.
Criteria: Must have >1500 endemic vascular plants and lost >70% habitat.
In India: Western Ghats, Himalayas, Indo-Burma, Sundaland (Nicobar).

3. Conservation Strategies

In-Situ (On Site)

Protecting species in their natural habitat.

Examples: National Parks (No human activity), Wildlife Sanctuaries (Limited activity), Biosphere Reserves, Sacred Groves.

Pros: Protects whole ecosystem. Cost effective for large scale.

Ex-Situ (Off Site)

Protecting endangered species outside their habitat. "Noah's Ark" approach.

Examples: Zoos, Botanical Gardens, Seed Banks, Cryopreservation (-196°C), Gene Banks.

Pros: Urgent protection for critical species. Breeding programs.

Case Study: Project Tiger (Success)

Launched: 1 april 1973 (Jim Corbett National Park).

Goal: To save the Bengal Tiger from extinction (Population had dropped to ~1,200 from 40,000).

Method: Tiger Reserve concept. Core-Buffer strategy. Core areas are inviolate (no human activity). Buffer areas allow limited use.

Result: Population doubled to nearly 3,167 (2022 census). India is home to 75% of the world's tigers. A global conservation success story.
