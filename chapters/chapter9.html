1. The Deep Dive: Insolation

Insolation is the primary source of energy for all atmospheric processes.

1.1 Factors Affecting Insolation

Factor Effect
Angle of Incidence (Solar Altitude) Higher angle (Sun overhead) = more concentrated energy = higher insolation. Maximum at tropics.
Duration of Daylight Longer days = more total energy received. Poles have 24-hour daylight during summer, but low intensity.
Transparency of Atmosphere Dust, clouds, and water vapor absorb/reflect solar radiation, reducing insolation reaching the surface.
Land vs. Water Water has a higher heat capacity. Heats and cools slower than land.
UPSC Critical: Aphelion & Perihelion

Earth's orbit is elliptical. It is closest to the Sun (Perihelion) around **January 3rd** and farthest (Aphelion) around **July 4th**. This seems counter-intuitive because January is winter in the Northern Hemisphere. This proves that seasons are caused by the tilt of Earth's axis, not distance from the Sun. [Source: NCERT]

2. Earth's Heat Budget

Despite constant insolation, Earth's average temperature remains relatively stable (~15°C). This is because the amount of energy received equals the amount of energy lost back to space. This balance is the Heat Budget.

Category Units (/100) Explanation
--- INCOMING SHORT-WAVE (Solar) ---
Total Incoming 100 Solar radiation reaching the top of the atmosphere.
--- OUTGOING (LOST) ---
Albedo (Reflected by Clouds/Surface) ~30 Reflected back to space, contributes no heat to Earth. (Short-wave)
Absorbed by Atmosphere ~20 Absorbed by gases (O₃, H₂O vapor, dust) in the atmosphere. (Short-wave)
Absorbed by Earth's Surface ~50 Heats the land and water. This is eventually re-radiated as long-wave (infrared) Terrestrial Radiation.
--- BALANCE ---
Total Outgoing (Reflected + Radiated) 100 Energy lost = Energy gained. Hence, equilibrium.

3. Distribution of Temperature

Isotherms

Isotherms are lines connecting points of equal temperature. They generally run parallel to latitude in the Southern Hemisphere (more ocean = uniform heating) but are irregular in the Northern Hemisphere (continental effect).

Temperature Inversion

Normally, temperature decreases with altitude. A Temperature Inversion is an *abnormal* condition where temperature *increases* with height. Traps pollutants near the surface (smog). Common in valleys and on clear, calm nights.

4. Connect the Dots: Interdisciplinary Links

Physics: Stefan-Boltzmann Law

The intensity of radiation emitted by a body is proportional to the fourth power of its temperature (E = σT⁴). This is why hotter objects (like the Sun) emit short-wave radiation, while cooler objects (like Earth) emit long-wave (infrared) radiation. This fundamental physics underpins the entire greenhouse effect.

Environmental Science: Global Warming

The enhanced Greenhouse Effect occurs because increased CO₂ and CH₄ trap more outgoing terrestrial radiation. The heat budget is thrown off balance. More energy is retained than lost, leading to a rise in global average temperature.
