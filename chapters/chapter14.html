1. The Deep Dive: Waves

Waves are oscillatory movements of water caused by energy transfer from wind. The water particles move in circles (orbital motion), not horizontally; it is *energy* that is transmitted.

  • Crest: Highest point of the wave.
  • Trough: Lowest point of the wave.
  • Wave Height: Vertical distance from trough to crest.
  • Wavelength: Horizontal distance from crest to crest.
  • Wave Period: Time for one complete wavelength to pass a point.

Breaking Waves: As a wave approaches shore and enters shallow water, the bottom drags on the seabed, slowing it down. The top overruns the bottom, causing the wave to "break" (surf).

2. Tides

Tides are the periodic rise (High Tide) and fall (Low Tide) of sea level caused by the gravitational pull of the Moon (primarily) and the Sun.

Tide Type Lunar-Solar Alignment Effect
Spring Tide Sun, Earth, Moon are aligned (New Moon / Full Moon – Syzygy). Gravitational forces combine. **Greatest tidal range** (highest high, lowest low).
Neap Tide Sun and Moon are at right angles to Earth (First/Third Quarter Moon – Quadrature). Gravitational forces partially cancel. **Least tidal range.**
UPSC Critical: Why Two Tidal Bulges?

There is a bulge on the side facing the Moon (due to direct gravitational pull), AND on the opposite side. The far-side bulge is caused by **inertia** (centrifugal force of the Earth-Moon system). The Earth is being pulled towards the Moon, but the water on the far side is "left behind." This is why most coasts experience ~2 high and 2 low tides per day (semidiurnal tide). [Source: NCERT]

3. Ocean Currents

Ocean Currents are large-scale, persistent, directional flows of seawater. They are like "rivers in the ocean."

Warm Currents

Originate in low latitudes and flow towards the poles. Carry warm water. Moderate the climate of adjacent coasts (make them warmer). Ex: Gulf Stream (warms Western Europe), North Atlantic Drift, Kuroshio Current.

Cold Currents

Originate in high latitudes and flow towards the Equator. Carry cold water. Make adjacent coasts cooler and drier (e.g., fog deserts). Ex: Canary Current, California Current, Labrador Current, Peru (Humboldt) Current.

Key Pattern: The Gyres

Major currents circulate in large closed loops called Gyres. They rotate **clockwise in the Northern Hemisphere** and **counter-clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere** due to the Coriolis effect. This also creates Warm currents on the western side of ocean basins and Cold currents on the eastern side.

4. Connect the Dots: Interdisciplinary Links

Astronomy: Tidal Locking

The gravitational interaction that causes tides also causes Tidal Locking. This is why we always see the same face of the Moon. Over billions of years, Earth-Moon tidal friction is also slowing Earth's rotation and pushing the Moon further away.

Biology: Upwelling & Fisheries

Where winds push surface water away from a coast (e.g., Peru coast), cold, nutrient-rich water rises from the deep (Upwelling). This supports massive plankton blooms, which form the base of a rich food chain, creating the world's most productive fisheries.
