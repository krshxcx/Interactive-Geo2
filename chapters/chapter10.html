10. Atmospheric Circulation

The global highway of winds.

"We live at the bottom of an ocean of air." — Evangelista Torricelli

Key Takeaways

  • Winds flow from high pressure to low pressure areas
  • Coriolis Effect: deflects winds right in NH, left in SH
  • Three wind cells: Hadley, Ferrel, Polar
  • Jet Streams are fast upper-atmosphere winds influencing weather patterns

1. The Forces of Wind

Pressure Gradient Force (PGF)

Driving Force: Air moves from High Pressure (HP) to Low Pressure (LP).
Steepness: Closer isobars = Stronger wind.

Coriolis Force

Deflecting Force: Caused by Earth's rotation.
Ferrel's Law: Wind deflects to the RIGHT in Northern Hemisphere and LEFT in Southern Hemisphere.
Magnitude: Zero at Equator (no deflection), Maximum at Poles.

Geostrophic Wind
When PGF and Coriolis Force are perfectly balanced, wind blows parallel to isobars. (Occurs in upper atmosphere).

2. The Tri-Cellular Model

The global circulation is divided into three distinct cells in each hemisphere.

Cell Name Latitude Wind System Mechanism
Hadley Cell 0° - 30° (Tropics) Trade Winds (Easterlies) Thermal: Air rises at Equator (ITCZ/Low), flows poleward, sinks at 30° (Subtropical High), flows back to Equator.
Ferrel Cell 30° - 60° (Mid-Lat) Westerlies Mechanical: Indirect, friction-driven cell. Air flows from Subtropical High (30°) to Subpolar Low (60°). Rises at 60° (Frontal Uplift).
Polar Cell 60° - 90° (Poles) Polar Easterlies Thermal: Cold dense air sinks at Poles (High), flows to 60° Low.

ITCZ (Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone)

The "Thermal Equator." Zone of intense low pressure where NE and SE Trade Winds collide. Shifts North in July (bringing Monsoon to India) and South in January.

Horse Latitudes (30° N/S)

Zone of sinking high pressure. Calm winds, dry air. Most of the world's hot deserts (Sahara, Atacama, Kalahari) are located here due to sinking dry air.

Jet Streams

Fast flowing, narrow air currents in the upper atmosphere. The Subtropical Jet Stream influences Indian Monsoons.

3. Atmosphere's Fury: Cyclones

Feature Tropical Cyclone Temperate (Extra-Tropical) Cyclone
Origin Thermal origin (Warm Oceans > 27°C). Frontal origin (Meeting of Warm & Cold air).
Location 8° - 20° Latitude (Tropics). 30° - 60° Latitude (Mid-Latitudes).
Shape Circular, with an "Eye" (Calm center). Inverted 'V' shape. No eye.
Movement East to West (Trade Winds). West to East (Westerlies).
Destruction Violent, destructive wind velocity. Short duration. Less violent. Brings widespread rain. Long duration.
Case Study: El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO)

Normal Year (La Niña Conditions)

Peru Current: Cold water off Peru coast (Upwelling).
Pressure: High Pressure over Tahiti (East Pacific), Low Pressure over Darwin (West Pacific).
Result: Rain in Australia/Indonesia. Good Monsoon in India.

El Niño Year

Current: Warm water replaces cold off Peru.
Pressure: Reversal. Low Pressure over Tahiti, High Pressure over Darwin.
Result: Drought in Australia/India. Floods in Peru.
