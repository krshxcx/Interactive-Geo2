1. Composition of the Atmosphere

The atmosphere is a mixture of gases, water vapor, and particulate matter held to the Earth by gravity. It is essential for all life.

1.1 Permanent Gases

Gas % by Volume Significance
Nitrogen (N₂) 78.08% Dilutes oxygen. Essential for life (amino acids, proteins). Fixed by bacteria (Nitrogen Cycle).
Oxygen (O₂) 20.95% Essential for respiration and combustion. Produced by photosynthesis.
Argon (Ar) 0.93% Inert gas. Used in light bulbs and welding.

1.2 Variable Gases & Particles (Crucial for UPSC)

Water Vapor (0-4%)

The most important variable gas. Concentrations decrease from Equator to Poles, and from surface to altitude. Acts as a Greenhouse Gas. Source of all precipitation.

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂ ~0.04%)

Primary anthropogenic greenhouse gas. Absorbs terrestrial (long-wave) radiation. Concentration has risen from ~280 ppm (pre-industrial) to over 420 ppm today. [Source: NOAA]

Ozone (O₃)

A form of oxygen found mainly in the Stratosphere (15-35 km). Absorbs harmful UV-B and UV-C radiation. At ground level, O₃ is a pollutant (smog component).

Dust Particles (Aerosols)

Include pollen, soot, volcanic ash, sea salt. Act as Hygroscopic Nuclei for condensation (cloud/rain formation). Scatter sunlight (causing red sunsets, blue sky).

2. Structure of the Atmosphere

The atmosphere is divided into layers based on the variation of temperature with altitude.

Troposphere (0 - ~12 km)

"The Weather Sphere." Contains ~75% of atmospheric mass and almost all water vapor and dust. Temperature decreases with altitude at the Normal Lapse Rate (6.5°C/km). The upper boundary is the **Tropopause**.

Stratosphere (~12 - 50 km)

"The Ozone Layer." Temperature *increases* with altitude due to absorption of UV by ozone. Very stable, no weather. Ideal for jet aircraft. Upper boundary: **Stratopause**.

Mesosphere (~50 - 80 km)

"The Coldest Layer." Temperature decreases again (to ~-100°C at top). Meteors burn up here ("shooting stars"). Upper boundary: **Mesopause**.

Thermosphere (~80 - 700+ km)

"The Ionized Layer." Temperature rises sharply (up to 1500°C), but feels cold due to extremely low air density. Contains the Ionosphere (important for radio communication). Auroras occur here.

Exosphere (~700+ km)

The outermost layer. Atmosphere gradually transitions into outer space. Gas molecules can escape Earth's gravity (Jeans escape).
