Heat Budget Module

9. Solar Radiation

The energetic currency of our planet.

"The Sun is the engine that drives the weather machine."

Key Takeaways

  • Earth receives solar energy as shortwave radiation; emits longwave (infrared)
  • Albedo = % of solar radiation reflected (fresh snow ≈ 80-90%, water ≈ 5-10%)
  • Insolation varies with latitude, season, and length of day
  • Heat budget: incoming solar = outgoing terrestrial radiation (balanced over time)

1. Insolation & Heating

Insolation

Shortwave Radiation: UV, Visible, Near-IR.
Mechanism: The atmosphere is largely transparent to incoming shortwaves.
Variability: Depends on:

  • Angle of Incidence: Direct rays (Equator) concentrate heat; Oblique rays (Poles) spread it out.
  • Duration of Day: Length of daylight hours.
  • Transparency: Cloud cover reflects heat.
  • Distance from Sun:
    • Perihelion: Closest to Sun (Jan 3rd). More heat.
    • Aphelion: Farthest from Sun (July 4th). Less heat.

Terrestrial Radiation

Longwave Radiation: Infrared (Thermal).
Mechanism: Earth absorbs shortwaves, heats up, and re-emits longwaves. The atmosphere (CO2, H2O) is opaque to longwaves (Greenhouse Effect).
Result: The atmosphere is heated from below, not from above.

📚 NCERT Data Vault

Atmosphere absorbs only 14% of direct insolation - primarily heated indirectly by Earth's long-wave radiation

Insolation varies by: Angle of incidence, Latitude, Altitude, Cloud cover

Heat Balance: Incoming solar radiation = Outgoing terrestrial radiation

2. Mechanisms of Heat Transfer

Mechanism Process Role in Atmosphere
Conduction Transfer through contact (Molecule to Molecule). Heats only the bottom few centimeters of air touching the hot ground.
Convection Vertical transfer (Rising currents). Major process. Heats the troposphere. Causes clouds and rain.
Advection Horizontal transfer (Wind). Dominant in middle latitudes. Causes heat waves and cold waves (Loo in India).

3. The Data Vault: Albedo

Definition: The percentage of solar radiation reflected back without heating the surface. (Global Mean Albedo = ~35%).

Surface Albedo (%) Implication
Fresh Snow 80% - 90% Very High. Keeps polar regions cold ("Snow-blindness").
Thick Clouds 70% - 80% Global cooling effect.
Sand / Desert 35% - 45% High. Deserts cool rapidly at night.
Grass / Crops 15% - 25% Moderate.
Asphalt / Concrete 5% - 10% Very Low. Absorbs massive heat (Urban Heat Island).
Ocean (Noon) 3% - 5% Very Low. Absorbs almost all energy (Heat Sink).
Critical Concept: Temperature Inversion

Usually, temperature drops with height (Normal Lapse Rate). Under special conditions, temperature increases with height.
Conditions: Long winter nights, calm air, clear skies.
Result: Cold air gets trapped below warm air. Causes Fog and concentrates pollutants (Smog). Common in valleys.

4. The Heat Budget

Input = Output
Earth maintains a constant temperature because Incoming Solar = Outgoing Terrestrial.

Income (100 units):

  • 35 units reflected back immediately (Albedo). [27 Clouds + 2 Ice + 6 Scattering]
  • 14 units absorbed by atmosphere (Ozone, Vapor).
  • 51 units absorbed by Earth's surface. [Total Heat Budget]

Expenditure (100 units):

  • 51 units radiated back by Earth:
    • 17 units escape directly to space (Radiation Window).
    • 34 units absorbed by atmosphere (Greenhouse).
  • 48 units (14 org + 34 abs) radiated by atmosphere to space.
  • Total Loss = 35 (Albedo) + 17 (Direct) + 48 (Atmos) = 100.
Case Study: Urban Heat Island (UHI)

Phenomenon: Cities are significantly warmer than surrounding rural areas (2°C - 5°C higher).

Causes:

  • Low Albedo: Concrete and asphalt absorb heat.
  • Structure: Tall buildings trap heat (Canyon Effect) and block wind.
  • Anthropogenic Heat: Vehicles, ACs, factories release heat.
  • Lack of Vegetation: Less transpiration (cooling).

Impact: Increased energy demand (ACs), heat stress, worse air quality.
