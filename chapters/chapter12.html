12. World Climate & Change

Classifying the planet's diverse environments.

"Climate is what you expect, weather is what you get." — Robert A. Heinlein

Key Takeaways

  • Köppen Classification: 5 major climate groups (A, B, C, D, E) + Highland (H)
  • Classification based on temperature and precipitation patterns
  • Without the greenhouse effect, Earth would be -18°C instead of +15°C
  • Key protocols: Kyoto (1997) and Paris Agreement (2015)

1. Köppen's Classification (1918)

Based on mean monthly temperature and precipitation. Uses letter codes.

Group Name Characteristics
A Tropical Humid Coldest month > 18°C. No winter. High rainfall.
B Dry Evaporation > Precipitation. Water deficit. (Desert/Steppe).
C Warm Temperate Coldest month between 18°C and -3°C. Mid-latitudes.
D Cold Snow Forest Coldest month < -3°C. Warmest> 10°C. Continental climates (Russia/Canada).
E Polar Warmest month < 10°C. No summer. Tundra/Ice Cap.
H Highland Climate due to altitude (Mountains). Temperature decreases with height.
The Small Letters (Precipitation)

f: No dry season (Rainforest).
m: Monsoon (Short dry season).
w: Winter dry season (Savanna - Aw).
s: Summer dry season (Mediterranean - Cs).

📚 NCERT Data Vault: Climate Change

Causes: Astronomical (sunspots, Milankovitch cycles), Terrestrial (volcanic eruptions), Human-induced (GHGs: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs)

Impacts: Rising CO₂, Ozone depletion (Antarctic hole), Sea-level rise (melting + thermal expansion)

2. Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming

The Greenhouse Effect (Natural)

Essential for life. Without it, Earth's average temperature would be -18°C instead of the current +15°C. Water vapor is the biggest natural contributor.

Global Warming (Enhanced)

Anthropogenic (Human-made) increase in GHGs traps too much heat.

Gas Source GWP (Potential)
CO2 Fossil fuels, Deforestation. 1 (Baseline)
Methane (CH4) Paddy fields, Livestock (Gut bacteria), Wetlands. 25x
Nitrous Oxide (N2O) Fertilizers, Nylon industry. 300x
CFCs/HFCs Refrigerants. Thousands x

Key Protocols

Kyoto Protocol (1997): First binding treaty to cut GHG emissions.
Paris Agreement (2015): Aim to keep global temp rise well below 2°C.

Case Study: The Amazon (Af) vs Sahara (BWh)

Amazon Basin (Af)

Climate: Tropical Wet (Rainforest). Rain every afternoon (4 PM Shower). No seasons.
Vegetation: Thick canopy, Epiphytes, Hardwood trees (Ebony, Mahogany).
Soil: Laterite (Leached, acidic).

Sahara Desert (BWh)

Climate: Subtropical Hot Desert.
Mechanism: Located under the sinking Subtropical High Pressure belt (30°N). Sinking air warms adiabatically and prevents cloud formation (Offshore Trade Winds).
Vegetation: Xerophytic (Cactus, Acacia).
