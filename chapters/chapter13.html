Oceanography Module

13. Water (Oceans)

The blue basin that covers 71% of Earth.

"The sea, once it casts its spell, holds one in its net of wonder forever." — Jacques Cousteau

Key Takeaways

  • Oceans cover 71% of Earth's surface and hold 97% of all water
  • Ocean floor: Continental Shelf → Slope → Rise → Abyssal Plain → Trenches
  • Average ocean temperature: ~17°C; salinity: 35‰ (parts per thousand)
  • Temperature and salinity decrease with depth; density increases

1. Ocean Floor Relief / Topography

The ocean floor is not flat. It is more rugged than land.

1. Continental Shelf (Shallow)

Depth: ~200m.
Significance: Sunlight reaches bottom (Photic Zone). Richest fishing banks (Grand Banks, Dogger Bank). Huge reserves of Oil & Gas (Mumbai High).

2. Continental Slope (Steep)

Gradient: Steep descent (2-5°).
Feature: Connects shelf to deep sea. Submarine Canyons (deep gorges cut by turbidity currents) are found here.

3. Deep Sea Plain (Abyssal Plain)

Depth: 3,000 - 6,000m.
Feature: Flattest areas on Earth. Covered in fine ooze (clay/silt). Manganese nodules found here.

4. Ocean Deeps (Trenches)

Feature: Deepest parts. Formed by Subduction (Convergent Boundaries).
Example: Mariana Trench (11km deep) in Pacific.

2. The Properties of Seawater

Property Horizontal Trend Vertical Trend
Temperature Decreases from Equator (27°C) to Poles (-2°C). Enclosed seas (Red Sea) are hotter. Decreases rapidly with depth in the Thermocline layer (200-1000m). Constant cold at bottom.
Salinity Highest at Tropics (Subtropical High) due to High Evap + Low Rain. Lower at Equator (Heavy Rain). Lowest at Poles (Ice melt). Increases with depth (at high latitudes). Distinct layer: Halocline (Rapid salinity increase).
Average Salinity: 35 ppt
35 parts per thousand (35g salt in 1kg water).
Composition: NaCl (77%), MgCl2 (11%).
Case Study: The Dead Sea (Extreme Salinity)

Location: Jordan / Israel / Palestine border.

Salinity: ~340 ppt (10x ocean average).

Reason: Landlocked lake with no outlet. High evaporation rates in desert heat concentrate the salts delivered by the Jordan River.

Effect: Density is so high that humans float effortlessly ("Antigravity" effect). No fish can survive.
