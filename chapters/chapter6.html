Geomorphology Module

6. Geomorphic Processes

The forces that build (Internal) and destroy (External) the Earth's surface.

"The present is the key to the past." (Uniformitarianism) — James Hutton

Key Takeaways

  • Geomorphic processes are either endogenic (internal) or exogenic (external)
  • Endogenic forces: Volcanism, Diastrophism, Earthquakes
  • Exogenic forces: Weathering, Erosion, Mass Wasting
  • Weathering is in-situ; erosion involves transport by wind, water, ice, or gravity

1. The Dual Forces

Earth's surface is uneven because of two opposing forces working continuously.

Endogenic (Internal) Forces

Source: Heat from radio-activity, rotational friction, and primordial heat.

Action: Constructive. They elevate land.

Examples: Diastrophism, Volcanism.

Exogenic (External) Forces

Source: Solar Energy (Sun) and Gravity.

Action: Destructive (Denudation). They wear down land.

Examples: Weathering, Mass Wasting, Erosion, Deposition.

2. Weathering

The disintegration and decomposition of rocks in situ (without transport).

Chemical Weathering

Oxidation: Reaction with Oxygen (Rusting). Common in Iron-rich rocks.
Carbonation: Reaction with CO2 (Carbonic Acid). Dissolves Limestone (Karst Topography).
Hydration: Absorbing water, expanding volume. Gypsum.

Physical Weathering

Unloading (Exfoliation): Removal of overlying rock weight causes expansion and peeling (Onion skin).
Frost Wedging: Water freezes in cracks, expands by 9%, and breaks rock.
Thermal Expansion: Daily heating/cooling cycles in deserts.

Biological Weathering

Roots of plants growing into cracks. Burrowing animals (earthworms, rodents). Human mining.

3. Mass Movements

Movement of rock debris down a slope under the direct influence of Gravity alone. (No water/wind transport needed).

Speed/Moisture Movement Type Description
Slow / Dry Creep Almost imperceptible. Tilted fences/poles are signs. Common on steep, grassy slopes.
Rapid / Wet Earth Flow / Mud Flow Saturated clay flowing down. "Lahars" are volcanic mudflows.
Catastrophic / Dry Rock Fall Free fall of blocks from a vertical face.
Rapid / Mixed Landslide Sliding of a coherent block. "Slump" creates backward rotation.
Case Study: Landslides (Himalayas vs Western Ghats)

The Himalayas (High Vulnerability)

Reason: Tectonically active (unstable), young sedimentary rocks (soft), steep slopes, glacial activity.

Events: Frequent massive slides like Kedarnath (2013).

The Western Ghats (Moderate Vulnerability)

Reason: Stable shield, hard rocks. But steep western slopes + high rainfall + road cutting.

Events: Pune (Malin) landslide (2014) due to heavy rain + deforestation.

4. Soil Formation (Pedogenesis)

Soil = f(P, C, O, R, T)
Soil is a function of: Parent Material, Climate, Organisms, Relief, Time. (Jenny's Equation)
Factor Role
Parent Material Determines texture, chemical properties. (Granite -> Sandy, Basalt -> Clayey)
Climate (Most Active) Temp & Rainfall control rate of weathering and humus formation.
Time Determines maturity. Older soils have distinct horizons (Profile).
