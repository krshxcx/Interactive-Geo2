1. The Deep Dive: Sources of Our Knowledge

The deepest borehole ever drilled (Kola Superdeep Borehole, Russia) reached only 12.26 km – a mere 0.2% of Earth's 6,371 km radius. Thus, our knowledge of the interior is almost entirely *indirect*.

1.1 Seismic Waves: The Ultimate Probe

Seismology is our primary tool. When an earthquake occurs, it releases energy in the form of waves. By analyzing how these waves travel through and around the Earth, we deduce its internal structure.

Property P-Waves (Primary) S-Waves (Secondary)
Type of Motion Longitudinal (Compressional – push-pull like sound) Transverse (Shear – side-to-side like light)
Media Travels through Solids, Liquids, and Gases. Solids Only. Cannot pass through liquids (crucial for detecting liquid outer core).
Velocity Faster (~8 km/s in mantle) Slower (~4.5 km/s in mantle)
Shadow Zone 103° to 142° from epicenter (caused by refraction at core-mantle boundary). All points beyond 103° from epicenter (caused by absorption by liquid outer core).
UPSC Critical: How Shadow Zones Reveal the Core

The S-Wave Shadow Zone (beyond 103°) is the smoking gun proof of a *liquid outer core*. S-waves cannot travel through liquids, so when they hit the outer core, they are absorbed. The distinct P-Wave Shadow Zone (103°-142°) is caused by P-waves bending (refracting sharply) as they pass from the solid mantle into the denser liquid core, creating a 'blind spot' on the opposite side of the Earth. 2. The Layered Earth: Structure & Composition

The Crust

Two Types: Continental (SIAL) – Silica/Aluminium (Granitic), thicker (30-70 km). Oceanic (SIMA) – Silica/Magnesium (Basaltic), thinner (5-10 km).

Key Discontinuity: The Conrad Discontinuity separates the upper (granitic) from lower (basaltic) continental crust.

The Mantle (~2,900 km thick)

Separated from the crust by the Moho Discontinuity. The upper mantle contains the Asthenosphere – a partially molten, weak zone that allows tectonic plates to "float" and move. Below it lies the rigid mesosphere.

The Core (NIFE: ~3,471 km radius)

Separated from the mantle by the Gutenberg Discontinuity. Outer Core: Liquid (Iron-Nickel). Convection currents here generate Earth's magnetic field. Inner Core: Solid (Fe-Ni). Despite being hotter than the outer core, immense pressure keeps it solid. The boundary is the Lehmann Discontinuity (~5,150 km).

4. Connect the Dots: Interdisciplinary Links

Physics: The Magnetic Field & Dynamo Theory

Earth's magnetic field protects us from the solar wind. It's generated by the **Geodynamo** – convective flow of the liquid iron-nickel outer core. This is an application of electromagnetic induction (Faraday's Law) on a planetary scale.

Chemistry: Why SIAL Floats on SIMA

Continental crust (rich in lighter elements: Si, Al, K) has a lower density (~2.7 g/cm³) than oceanic crust (richer in denser elements: Si, Mg, Fe) (~3.0 g/cm³). This density difference, rooted in geochemistry, is why continental crust is buoyant and rarely subducts.
