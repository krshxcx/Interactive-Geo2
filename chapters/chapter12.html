2. Climate Change: Causes and Evidence

Natural Causes

  • Astronomical Variations (Milankovitch Cycles): Variations in Earth's orbital eccentricity, axial tilt (obliquity), and precession of equinoxes affect the amount/distribution of insolation over 10,000-100,000 year cycles. Believed to trigger ice ages.
  • Volcanism: Large eruptions inject aerosols (sulfur dioxide) into the stratosphere, blocking sunlight and causing short-term (1-3 year) cooling. Ex: Mt. Pinatubo (1991) cooled Earth by ~0.5Â°C.
  • Solar Output: The Sun's energy output varies slightly (~0.1%) over the ~11-year sunspot cycle.

Anthropogenic (Human) Causes

  • Enhanced Greenhouse Effect: Burning Fossil Fuels releases COâ‚‚. Deforestation reduces COâ‚‚ sinks. Agriculture releases CHâ‚„ (rice paddies, cattle) and Nâ‚‚O. These gases enhance the natural greenhouse effect, trapping more heat.
  • Aerosols: Some aerosols (sulfates) have a cooling effect (reflect sunlight). Others (black carbon/soot) have a warming effect (absorb sunlight). The net effect is complex.
  • Land Use Change: Deforestation, urbanization change surface albedo and moisture fluxes, affecting local and regional climate.

3. Impacts of Global Warming

  • Sea Level Rise: Thermal expansion of water + melting of glaciers/ice sheets (Greenland, Antarctica). Threatens low-lying coastal areas and island nations (e.g., Maldives, Tuvalu, Bangladesh delta).
  • Extreme Weather Events: Increase in intensity and frequency of heatwaves, droughts, floods, and more powerful tropical cyclones.
  • Ecosystem Disruption: Changes in species distribution, coral bleaching (due to warmer oceans), loss of Arctic sea ice (polar bears).
  • Impact on Agriculture: Shift in growing seasons, changes in crop yields (positive in some areas, negative in others), increased water stress.

4. Connect the Dots: Interdisciplinary Links

Environmental Science & Policy

The IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) synthesizes the science. International agreements like the Paris Agreement (2015) aim to limit warming. Understanding climate is foundational to environmental governance.

Economics: Climate Finance

Climate change is a massive economic issue. Concepts like Carbon Tax, Carbon Trading (Cap and Trade), and the debate over "Common but Differentiated Responsibilities" (CBDR) are central to climate negotiations. Adaptation and Mitigation require massive investment.
