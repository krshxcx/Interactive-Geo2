1. The Deep Dive: Competing Origin Theories

Understanding Earth's origin requires navigating a landscape of evolving hypotheses. Each theory reflects its era's scientific capabilities and suffered specific criticisms that led to its refinement or rejection. For UPSC Mains, knowing the *criticism* is as vital as the *mechanism*.

1.1 The Nebular Hypothesis (Monistic Theories)

Proponents: Immanuel Kant (1755) and Pierre-Simon Laplace (1796).

The Mechanism: A hot, gaseous, rotating Nebula slowly cooled and contracted. As it contracted, it spun faster (conservation of angular momentum). This faster spin caused it to flatten into a disc. From this disc, successive rings of gaseous matter were thrown off, which then condensed to form planets. The central mass became the Sun.

UPSC Critical: The Fatal Flaw

The Criticism (Angular Momentum Problem): If the Sun and planets formed from the same nebula, the angular momentum should be distributed according to mass. The Sun, holding 99.8% of the solar system's mass, should possess almost all the angular momentum. However, planets (especially Jupiter) hold ~98% of the system's angular momentum. This discrepancy is *unexplainable* by the classical Nebular Hypothesis and is why it was abandoned in its original form. [Source: Dayal]

1.2 The Tidal / Encounter Hypotheses (Dualistic Theories)

These theories arose to overcome the angular momentum problem. They propose an *external agent* transferring momentum to the forming planets.

Theory Proponent Mechanism Criticism
Planetesimal Hypothesis Chamberlin & Moulton (1904) A passing star caused a tidal eruption on the Sun. The ejected material formed Planetesimals which later aggregated into planets. Gases would disperse into space due to expansion, not condense.
Tidal Hypothesis James Jeans (1919) A massive star passed close enough to draw out a "cigar-shaped" filament of solar material. The bulging ends formed the large planets (Jupiter, Saturn), the thinner middle formed smaller ones. Statistically, such close encounters are impossibly rare. Also, hot ejected gas cannot condense into solids.
Binary Star Hypothesis H.N. Russell (1937) Our Sun was a binary star system. A third passing star collided with and disrupted the Sun's partner, creating debris that formed planets. Violates laws of thermodynamics; cannot explain planetary composition differences.

1.3 The Modern Paradigm: Big Bang to Solar Nebula

Proponent: The Big Bang was formalized by Georges Lemaître (1927) and supported observationally by Edwin Hubble's redshift observations (1929). [Source: NCERT]

The Mechanism (The Modern Synthesis):

  • ~13.8 Billion Years Ago (BYA): The universe began from a Singularity. Space and time itself were created in this event.
  • First 3 Minutes: The universe was a plasma of elementary particles. As it cooled, protons and neutrons formed, leading to Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (creation of H, He, Li).
  • ~300,000 Years Later: Universe cooled to ~3000K. Electrons could bind to nuclei, making the universe transparent. This era's light is visible today as the CMBR.
  • ~9 BYA (Solar Nebula Stage): A cloud of H/He gas (enriched by heavier elements from prior supernovae – "recycled stardust") collapsed. The Solar Nebular Disk Model (SNDM) now explains planet formation.
  • ~4.6 BYA: The Sun ignited. Within the protoplanetary disk, dust grains collided and stuck (accretion), forming planetesimals, then protoplanets, then planets. [Source: USGS]

3. Connect the Dots: Interdisciplinary Links

A "Gold Standard" answer integrates knowledge from multiple domains. Here's how physics and chemistry underpin Earth's origin.

Physics: Angular Momentum Conservation

As the solar nebula collapsed, its rotation rate increased. This is directly derived from the Law of Conservation of Angular Momentum (L = Iω). As the cloud's radius (I) decreased, its angular velocity (ω) had to increase. This is why the planets orbit in the same direction and (mostly) on the same plane – they inherited the original spin of the cloud.

Chemistry: Elemental Differentiation

Why is Earth layered? When the planet was molten, the process of Planetary Differentiation occurred. Heavy siderophile elements (Fe, Ni, Co) sank to form the core. Lighter lithophile elements (O, Si, Al, K) floated to form the crust and mantle. This is a direct application of density stratification and chemical affinity principles.

Astrophysics: The Goldilocks Zone

Earth's liquid water is only possible because it orbits within the Circumstellar Habitable Zone (the "Goldilocks Zone"). Too close (Venus) = runaway greenhouse. Too far (Mars) = frozen. This directly links planetary formation physics to astrobiology.
