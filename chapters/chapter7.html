Geomorphology Module

7. Landforms & Their Evolution

The sculptors of the Earth: Water, Ice, Wind, and Waves.

"Landscape is a function of structure, process, and stage." — W.M. Davis (Cycle of Erosion)

Key Takeaways

  • Landforms are shaped by running water, wind, glaciers, and waves
  • River stages: Youth (V-valleys), Mature (meanders), Old (floodplains, deltas)
  • Glacial landforms: Cirques, U-valleys, Moraines, Drumlins
  • Karst topography forms in limestone regions: sinkholes, caves, stalactites

1. The Erosion Cycle (Davis)

Landforms evolve through identifiable stages of life.

Stage River Characteristics Landform Features
Youth Fast, vertical erosion (down-cutting). V-shape valleys. Gorges, Canyons, Waterfalls. No floodplains.
Mature Widening of valley (lateral erosion). Meandering starts. Floodplains, Alluvial Fans, Meanders.
Old Slow flow. Deposition dominates. Ox-bow lakes, Deltas, Peneplains (flat featureless plains).

📚 NCERT Data Vault

Erosional Landforms: V-shaped valleys, gorges, canyons (running water) | Cirques, horns, arêtes, U-valleys (glaciers)

Depositional Landforms: Flood plains, deltas (water) | Barchans, parabolic, seif dunes (wind) | Moraines, eskers, drumlins (glaciers)

2. The Agents of Erosion

A. Running Water (Fluvial)

Erosional: Valleys (V-shaped), Gorges (steep), Canyons (stepped), Pot holes, Meanders (cut banks).
Depositional: Alluvial Fans (mountains to plains), Deltas (Bird-foot, Arcuate), Floodplains, Natural Levees.

B. Groundwater (Karst/Limestone)

Mechanism: Slightly acidic rainwater dissolves calcium carbonate rocks.
Erosional: Sinkholes, Lapies, Caves.
Depositional: Stalactites (ceiling), Stalagmites (floor), Pillars (connected).

C. Glaciers (Ice)

Erosional: Cirque (armchair), Arete (ridge), Horn (peak), U-shaped Valley (Trough), Hanging Valley.
Depositional: Moraines (Lateral, Medial, Terminal), Eskers (ridges), Drumlins (inverted boat), Outwash Plains.

D. Wind (Aeolian - Deserts)

Erosional: Mushroom Rocks, Inselbergs, Zeugens, Yardangs.
Depositional: Sand Dunes (Barchan/Crescent, Seif/Linear), Loess (fine dust).

Case Study: The Grand Canyon, USA

Agent: Running Water (The Colorado River).

Type: A Canyon (Stepped valley sides), unlike a Gorge (Steep vertical sides).

Formation: The river cut down through layers of sedimentary rock while the Colorado Plateau was being slowly uplifted. It exposes nearly 2 Billion years of Earth's history.
