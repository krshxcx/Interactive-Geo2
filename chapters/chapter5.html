5. Minerals & Rocks

The building blocks of the Earth's Crust.

"Rocks are records of events that took place at the time they were formed. They are books of history." — John Wesley Powell

Key Takeaways

  • Minerals are naturally occurring, inorganic, solid with a definite chemical composition
  • Three rock types: Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic
  • The Rock Cycle shows continuous transformation between rock types
  • Feldspar is the most abundant mineral group in Earth's crust

1. The Atomic Foundation: Minerals

A mineral is a natural, inorganic substance with a definite atomic structure and chemical composition.

Earth's Crust Composition

98% of the crust is made of just 8 elements:

  • Oxygen (46.6%)
  • Silicon (27.7%)
  • Aluminum (8.1%)
  • Iron (5.0%)
  • Calcium (3.6%), Sodium (2.8%), Potassium (2.6%), Magnesium (2.1%).

Major Mineral Groups

Silicates: Feldspar (most abundant), Quartz (most durable).

Non-Silicates: Carbonates (Calcite), Sulfides (Pyrite), Oxides (Hematite).

Metallic vs Non-Metallic Minerals

Metallic: Precious (Gold), Ferrous (Iron), Non-Ferrous (Copper).
Non-Metallic: Sulfur, Phosphates, Nitrate, Cement.

📚 NCERT Data Vault: Mohs Hardness Scale

Quick reference: Talc (1 - softest) → Gypsum (2) → Calcite (3) → Fluorite (4) → Apatite (5) → Orthoclase (6) → Quartz (7 - scratches glass) → Topaz (8) → Corundum (9 - ruby/sapphire) → Diamond (10 - hardest)

NCERT Rock Cycle:
  • Igneous (primary rocks from magma) → Weathering → Sedimentary
  • Any rock + Heat/Pressure → Metamorphic
  • Any rock → Subduction/Melting → Magma → Igneous
  • 98% of Crust: O-46.6%, Si-27.7%, Al-8.1%, Fe-5%, Ca, Na, K, Mg (rest)

2. The Data Vault: Physical Properties

Geologists identify minerals by specific physical traits.

Property Definition Example
Hardness (Mohs Scale) Resistance to scratching. Talc (1) vs Diamond (10).
Cleavage Tendency to break along flat planes. Mica (Perfect sheets).
Fracture Irregular breakage. Quartz (Conchoidal fracture).
Lustre How light reflects. Metallic (Gold), Glassy (Quartz), Silky (Gypsum).
Streak Color of powder form. Malachite (Green), Hematite (Red).

3. The Rock Cycle

Rocks are aggregates of minerals. They transform over geological time.

Igneous Rocks (The Primary Rocks)

Formed from cooling Magma/Lava. No fossils. Crystalline.
Intrusive: Granite (Slow cooling, large crystals).
Extrusive: Basalt (Fast cooling, fine crystals).

Sedimentary Rocks (Secondary)

Formed from Lithification (compaction) of sediments. Distinct layers (strata). Contains fossils.
Mechanically Formed: Sandstone, Shale.
Organically Formed: Limestone (Coral/Shells), Coal.
Chemically Formed: Gypsum, Salt.

Metamorphic Rocks (Changed Form)

Formed under Heat, Pressure, & Volume (PVT) changes. Recrystallization occurs.
Granite → Gneiss
Clay/Shale → Slate
Limestone → Marble
Coal → Graphite/Diamond

Case Study: The Deccan Traps

Location: Western India (Maharashtra, Gujarat, MP).

Formation: A classic example of Flood Basalt Provinces. Around 66 Million Years ago (Cretaceous end), a massive volcanic eruption poured lava over 500,000 km².

Result: Layer upon layer of basalt (steps/traps) formed. Weathering of this basalt created the Black Cotton Soil (Regur), crucial for India's cotton agriculture.
